The tale of a mass on a spring

Our creative representation tells the whole project as a short comic: the question we asked, the forces at play, the four ways the motion can settle, and why any of it matters in the real world.

1

The question. Pull the mass down, let go, and ask: how does it come to rest?

2

The forces. The spring pulls back, the damper drains energy, the mass holds its motion. Together they give one equation.

3

Undamped (c = 0). With no damper, it bounces forever and never settles.

4

Underdamped. It overshoots a few times, each smaller than the last, then rests.

5

Critically damped. Just enough damping for the fastest return to rest, with no bounce at all.

6

Overdamped. Too much damping, so it crawls back to rest slowly without overshooting.

7

The choice. Engineers tune the damping to land in the right spot for the job.

8

The payoff. Good shock absorbers put a car near that sweet spot, so it glides over bumps instead of bouncing.
